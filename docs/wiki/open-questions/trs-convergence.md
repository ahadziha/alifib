# Does the TRS encoding preserve confluence / termination / convergence?

`examples/TRS.ali` opens by claiming it "simulates the dynamics of term
rewriting, preserving confluence/termination/convergence on closed terms." This
is the headline property of the [[trs-encoding]], and it is **believed but
unproved** — nothing in the repository states it formally or checks it.

## What it cannot naively mean

It is not the claim that the combined directed relation $R \cup E$ terminates.
The structural theory $E$ (the cells of `TRS.ali`) is deliberately **not**
terminating as an unrestricted rewriting system: with no
[[0001-no-identities|identity cells]], an isomorphism is presented as two
opposing cells, so `Sort.lunit_inv : wire ⟶ unit wire` and
`Sort.lunit : unit wire ⟶ wire` form a 2-cycle on any wire (likewise
`runit`/`runit_inv`). A faithful statement must therefore be about $R$ rewriting
**modulo** the structural congruence generated by $E$, restricted to **closed**
(ground) terms — and *which* such statement is the right one is itself not
pinned down in-repo.

## What a proof would have to establish

Three obligations, none currently discharged:

1. **Adequacy of the encoding.** Closed terms of a sort correspond to
   ($E$-normal) closed 2-diagrams, and each one-step $R$-rewrite of a term
   corresponds to a rewrite of its diagram modulo $E$ — a soundness/completeness
   pair making "run the diagram" agree with "run the TRS."
2. **Convergence of $E$ on closed terms.** The structural cells must let any
   closed diagram be normalised to a canonical term shape, with $E$-equivalence
   decidable — the part that would lean on the special-Frobenius normal form
   (`Unit`'s `merge`/`split` spiders) and the cartesian/Fox coherence
   (`copy`/`discard` naturality). The [[trs-encoding|deliberate absence]] of the
   bare comonoid laws (coassociativity, cocommutativity, counit) is a *bet* that
   on closed terms the naturality cells alone drive every `copy`/`discard` down
   to the constants — where `Constant.Copy_Nat` / `Discard_Nat` absorb them — so
   those laws are never needed as moves. Justifying that bet is part of this
   obligation.
3. **Transfer.** Given (1) and (2): if $R$ is confluent / terminating as a TRS,
   then so is $R \cup E$ modulo $E$ on closed terms.

## What is established

Only empirically. The encoding elaborates and *computes*: `BinaryNat` runs
arithmetic — `Eval.(run auto on parse "57 * (12 + 30)" end)` reduces to the
binary numeral — so on worked closed inputs the simulation produces the right
answer. That is evidence the dynamics are faithful, not a proof of any of
(1)–(3), and there is no test asserting confluence or termination of either
layer.

## What is at stake

If the simulation is unfaithful, "executing a TRS in alifib" could silently
diverge from the TRS's real behaviour — terminating where the TRS loops, or
reaching a different normal form — with nothing flagging it, since the engine
treats $E$ and $R$ as one undifferentiated rule set ([[trs-encoding]]) and never
checks the property.

## Possible resolutions

- Fix the precise statement first: name the rewriting **strategy** (e.g. orient
  a confluent-terminating fragment of $E$ and treat the unitor isomorphisms as a
  congruence rewritten *modulo*), then prove (1)–(3) against it.
- Borrow the machinery: Fox 1976 for the cartesian coherence, special-Frobenius
  normal-form results for the unit algebra, and rewriting-modulo / coherent
  rewriting (Huet; Lafont; Mimram) for the transfer — adapted to the *directed*,
  identity-free setting, which is where the standard results do not apply
  off-the-shelf.
- Add behavioural evidence in the meantime: tests pinning that chosen closed
  terms reach their expected normal forms, so a regression in the structural
  cells is caught even before the theory is settled.
